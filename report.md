# Executive Summary

Nexora Technologies Group delivered strong performance across all four quarters of 2026, achieving record revenue of **$5,847M**, EBITDA of **$1,287M**, and net income of **$612M**. Free cash flow surpassed the $700M threshold for the first time in company history. Growth was driven by accelerated adoption of our cloud infrastructure platform, international expansion into Southeast Asia, and disciplined cost management across all business units.

The board approved a $250M share buyback programme in Q3 2026 and raised the full-year dividend by 18% to $1.42 per share, reflecting confidence in the durability of our earnings profile.

From a strategic standpoint, 2026 validated the multi-year investment cycle the Group initiated in 2023. R&D expenditure of $543M funded thirteen new product capabilities across the Cloud Services and Enterprise Software segments, six of which reached general availability by year-end and collectively contributed approximately $180M in incremental annual contract value. The acquisition of DataVault Inc. in May reinforced the Group's data governance and compliance offering — an area of accelerating enterprise demand — at an attractive entry valuation of 4.8x forward revenue.

Margin expansion was structural rather than opportunistic. Gross margin improvement of 150 basis points reflects a sustained shift in revenue mix toward higher-margin recurring cloud subscriptions, combined with procurement efficiency gains under the Group's three-year vendor consolidation programme. General and administrative costs as a percentage of revenue declined for the third consecutive year, reaching 5.8% — evidence that the Group's shared services transformation is delivering the intended scale benefits.

The Group's capital allocation framework prioritised deleveraging, progressive dividends, and opportunistic buybacks, in that order. Net debt of $1,204M at year-end represents a leverage ratio of 0.94x EBITDA, ahead of the 1.0x internal target and well within all debt covenants. The Board considers the current capital structure appropriately conservative given the macro environment, while remaining flexible enough to pursue bolt-on acquisitions in the $50–200M range without requiring external financing.

## 2.3 Recurring vs. Non-Recurring Revenue

The sustained growth in recurring revenue is one of the most significant structural improvements in the Group's business model achieved over the past three years. In 2024, recurring revenue represented 72.7% of total Group revenue. By 2026, that proportion had grown to 75.1%, with recurring revenue expanding at a three-year CAGR of 18.4% compared to 5.1% for non-recurring revenue. Management's revenue quality objective is to reach 80% recurring by the end of 2028, driven primarily by the conversion of the remaining Enterprise Software perpetual licence base to subscription and the growth of multi-year Cloud Services contracts.

The Group defines **Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)** as:

$$
\text{ARR} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( \text{MRR}_i \times 12 \right)
$$

where $\text{MRR}_i$ is the Monthly Recurring Revenue for contract $i$.

# 4. Profitability & Margin Analysis

Profitability improvement is one of the Group's four primary long-term financial objectives, alongside revenue growth, free cash flow conversion, and leverage reduction. Management targets EBITDA margins in the range of 24–26% by 2028, representing approximately 200–400 basis points of expansion from the 2026 baseline. The primary levers are continued mix shift toward Cloud Services, which carries a segment EBITDA margin of 31% versus 12% for Hardware; sustained procurement efficiency; and operating leverage on the fixed-cost base as the Group scales.

## 4.2 Return Metrics

All three primary return metrics improved substantially in 2026, reflecting the compounding effect of higher earnings on a relatively stable asset base. ROCE of 18.1% exceeded the Group's weighted average cost of capital of approximately 9.4%, indicating that the Group is generating meaningful economic value above its cost of capital — a key criterion for the Board's assessment of strategic investment decisions. The spread between ROCE and WACC of approximately 870 basis points is the widest in the Group's listed history and is expected to widen further as the asset base continues to generate increasing returns from prior-year capital deployment.

ROE of 23.7% reflects the growing profitability of the equity base combined with the benefit of financial leverage at sub-1.0x net debt/EBITDA — a level that the Board considers optimal given the Group's resilient free cash flow profile and investment-grade credit rating.

$$

$$

$$

$$

$$

# 5. Capital Structure & Leverage

The Group's capital structure is governed by a formal Treasury Policy approved by the Board, which sets limits on net leverage, minimum liquidity, interest rate exposure, and counterparty credit risk. The Group maintains a target net debt/EBITDA range of 0.5–1.5x through the cycle, with a preference for the lower half of that range during periods of macro uncertainty. The Board reviews the capital structure annually and any material deviation from policy requires prior Board approval.

At December 31, 2026, the Group's credit ratings were Baa1 (Moody's) and BBB+ (S&P), both with stable outlooks. The investment-grade ratings provide access to the public bond markets at competitive rates and reduce refinancing risk. The Group's committed revolving credit facility of $600M was undrawn at year-end.

## 5.2 Debt Maturity Profile

The Group actively manages its debt maturity ladder to avoid cliff-edge refinancing risk. No single year accounts for more than 23% of total gross debt, and the weighted average maturity of the debt portfolio is 3.8 years (2025: 3.2 years). The 2027 maturity of $304M — the near-term tranche — is expected to be refinanced in H1 2027 via a new public bond issuance or drawdown under the revolving credit facility, depending on prevailing market conditions. Management has received preliminary indications from its relationship banks that refinancing terms broadly consistent with or below current coupon rates are achievable given current credit spreads.

The Group has no exposure to floating-rate debt; all borrowings carry fixed coupons. Interest rate risk on the debt portfolio is therefore limited to refinancing risk on future issuances. The Group periodically reviews whether to introduce interest rate hedging but currently considers its fixed-rate position appropriate given the forward interest rate curve.

# 6. Quarterly Deep Dive

## 6.1 Q1 2026 — January to March

Q1 2026 revenue of **$1,312M** grew 12.4% year-on-year, in line with guidance of 11–13%. Cloud Services accelerated to 21.8% YoY growth following the successful migration of 140 enterprise clients onto the Nexora Cloud Platform v4. EBITDA margin of 21.2% was 80 bps below the prior quarter due to seasonally higher payroll costs in January and planned product launch expenditure.

The quarter began with strong pipeline conversion from signings carried forward at the end of Q4 2025. Sales productivity improved measurably, with average deal size reaching $1.84M, up from $1.61M in Q1 2025. The Cloud & Infrastructure segment signed its largest-ever single contract in January — a five-year managed cloud services agreement with a North American insurance group valued at $312M in total contract value. Professional Services utilisation rates averaged 78.4% in the quarter, above the 76% full-year target, reflecting disciplined capacity planning and reduced bench time following the introduction of dynamic resourcing tools in late 2025.

Operating expenditure increased 10.2% year-on-year, below the revenue growth rate, reflecting early evidence of the operating leverage the Group expects to characterise the next phase of its growth. The primary cost growth was in the sales function (+14.1%), where the Group invested in market coverage expansion in Germany, the Netherlands, and Australia. Finance costs were $21M in Q1, and the effective tax rate of 29.8% was consistent with full-year guidance of approximately 30%.

## 6.2 Q2 2026 — April to June

Q2 delivered sequential momentum with revenue of **$1,448M**, the strongest Q2 in the Group's history. Enterprise Software renewals were exceptionally strong, with a net revenue retention rate of **118%**. The Group completed the acquisition of DataVault Inc. for $41M in cash, contributing approximately $12M to Q2 revenue.

# 8. Notes to the Financial Statements

## Note A — Revenue Recognition Policy

Revenue is recognised in accordance with IFRS 15 *Revenue from Contracts with Customers*. For multi-element arrangements, the transaction price is allocated to each performance obligation using the **Relative Standalone Selling Price (RSSP)** method:

$$
P_i = \frac{\text{SSP}_i}{\sum_{j=1}^{n} \text{SSP}_j} \times \text{Transaction Price}
$$

where $P_i$ is the allocated price for performance obligation $i$, and $\text{SSP}_i$ is its standalone selling price.

Subscription revenue is recognised ratably over the contract term. Professional services revenue is recognised as services are rendered using the input method (hours incurred / total estimated hours).

The Group applies the practical expedient in IFRS 15.121(a) for contracts with a duration of one year or less, and therefore does not disclose the transaction price allocated to remaining performance obligations for such contracts. For contracts with a duration exceeding one year, the aggregate amount of the transaction price allocated to performance obligations that are unsatisfied (or partially unsatisfied) at December 31, 2026 was $4,389M (2025: $3,622M), reflecting the growth in multi-year cloud subscriptions. Of this amount, $2,103M is expected to be recognised as revenue in the year ending December 31, 2027, and the remainder in subsequent periods.

Contract acquisition costs, representing incremental costs of obtaining a contract (primarily sales commissions), are capitalised and amortised over the expected contract life. The total balance of capitalised contract costs at December 31, 2026 was $182M (2025: $161M), and amortisation charged in the year was $74M (2025: $63M). No impairment of contract cost assets was recognised during the year.

## Note B — Goodwill & Impairment Testing

Goodwill of **$1,614M** (Dec-26) is allocated to two cash-generating units (CGUs):

| CGU                    | Goodwill (USDm) | Discount Rate | Headroom |
| ---------------------- | --------------: | ------------: | -------: |
| Cloud & Infrastructure |             986 |         10.2% |  $1,842M |
| Enterprise Solutions   |             628 |         11.4% |  $1,104M |
| **Total**        | **1,614** |               |          |

The recoverable amount for each CGU was determined using a **discounted cash flow (DCF)** model with a five-year explicit forecast period and terminal value:

$$
\text{Enterprise Value} = \sum_{t=1}^{5} \frac{\text{FCF}_t}{(1+\text{WACC})^t} + \frac{\text{FCF}_5 \times (1+g)}{(\text{WACC} - g)(1+\text{WACC})^5}
$$

where $g = 2.5\%$ (long-term nominal growth rate) and WACC = 10.2–11.4%. No impairment was recognised in 2026.

Sensitivity analysis was performed on the key assumptions underpinning the impairment test. For the Cloud & Infrastructure CGU, a 200 basis point increase in WACC would reduce the recoverable amount by approximately $420M but would not result in an impairment charge, given the $1,842M of headroom above carrying value. For the Enterprise Solutions CGU, a sustained 3% reduction in revenue growth relative to the base case, combined with a 100 basis point WACC increase, would reduce the headroom to approximately $310M — still significantly above carrying value. Management is therefore satisfied that no reasonably possible change in key assumptions would give rise to an impairment charge in either CGU.

Goodwill of $30M arising from the acquisition of DataVault Inc. was allocated to the Cloud & Infrastructure CGU, consistent with management's expectation that the significant majority of DataVault's commercial synergies will be realised within that segment's customer base and product platform.
